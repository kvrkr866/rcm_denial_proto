TITLE: Prior Authorization Appeal Procedure
SOURCE: SOP-AUTH-002
CARC_CODES: 97, 96, 167, 4
PAYER_IDS: BCBS, AETNA, CIGNA

OVERVIEW:
Prior authorization denials occur when a service requiring pre-approval was performed
without obtaining authorization, or when the authorization was not properly referenced
on the claim. CARC 97 indicates the benefit is included in another adjudicated service.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE:

1. VERIFY AUTHORIZATION STATUS
   - Check EHR prior auth module for existing authorization
   - Verify authorization covers the exact CPT code(s) billed
   - Confirm authorization was active on the date of service
   - Verify correct authorization number was listed on the claim

2. IF AUTHORIZATION EXISTS BUT WAS NOT CITED
   - Correct and resubmit claim with authorization number in field 23 (CMS-1500) or FL-63 (UB-04)
   - Include authorization approval letter as attachment
   - This is a corrected claim, not an appeal

3. IF AUTHORIZATION WAS NOT OBTAINED
   a) Submit retrospective/retro-authorization request immediately
   b) Simultaneously prepare appeal letter stating clinical necessity

4. CLINICAL DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED FOR APPEAL
   - Complete physician progress notes
   - Diagnosis justification and history
   - Failed conservative treatment documentation
   - Applicable clinical guidelines
   - Letter of Medical Necessity (physician-signed, on letterhead)

5. ESCALATION
   - If Level 1 appeal denied: file Level 2 appeal
   - Consider peer-to-peer review request with payer medical director

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS CHECKLIST:
[ ] Appeal letter (physician co-signed preferred)
[ ] Authorization approval letter (if exists)
[ ] Encounter note / operative report
[ ] Letter of Medical Necessity
[ ] Clinical guidelines supporting procedure
[ ] Original EOB
