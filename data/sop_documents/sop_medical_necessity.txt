TITLE: Medical Necessity Documentation and Appeal Guide
SOURCE: SOP-MN-003
CARC_CODES: 50, 55, 167, 170
PAYER_IDS: MEDICARE, BCBS, AETNA, CIGNA

OVERVIEW:
Medical necessity denials indicate the payer determined the service was not medically
necessary based on submitted information. These require strong clinical documentation
and often a physician-signed appeal letter citing clinical guidelines.

CLINICAL DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:

1. PHYSICIAN DOCUMENTATION
   - Complete encounter note describing patient's chief complaint
   - Relevant medical history and physical examination findings
   - Assessment and plan clearly linking diagnosis to the procedure
   - Documentation of failed conservative/alternative treatments
   - Physician attestation of medical necessity

2. DIAGNOSTIC SUPPORT
   - Relevant lab results, imaging reports, pathology reports
   - Results must directly support the diagnosis codes billed
   - Include dates and interpreting physician names

3. TREATMENT HISTORY
   - Document prior treatment attempts (medications, physical therapy, etc.)
   - Show progression of condition requiring the billed service
   - Reference time period of conservative treatment failure

4. CLINICAL GUIDELINES REFERENCES
   For Medicare: Reference applicable LCD (Local Coverage Determination)
   - Find applicable LCD at: cms.gov/medicare-coverage-database
   For Commercial Payers: Reference payer's clinical coverage policy

5. LETTER OF MEDICAL NECESSITY
   Required elements:
   - Patient name and member ID
   - Date of service and procedure(s)
   - Primary diagnosis and supporting diagnoses
   - Clinical rationale for the specific procedure
   - Why alternative treatments were insufficient
   - Physician signature and NPI

APPEAL STRUCTURE:
Paragraph 1: Formal request for reconsideration
Paragraph 2: Brief clinical summary
Paragraph 3: Clinical justification with guideline citations
Paragraph 4: Documentation inventory
Paragraph 5: Request for reversal and expedited review

MEDICARE-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE:
- Reference CMS IOM Publication 100-02, Chapter 15 for covered services
- For inpatient denials, reference Two-Midnight Rule (42 CFR 412.3)
- File Redetermination within 120 days of initial denial notice
